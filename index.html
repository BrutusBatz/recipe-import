Garlic Confit & Caramelized Onion Pasta

Serves 2 generously or 3 smaller portions.

Ingredients

  • 200–250 g pasta
  • 1 whole head garlic
  • Olive oil, enough to cover the garlic cloves
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 tbsp chili flakes or chili paste
  • 3–4 tbsp sun-dried tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan, plus extra for serving
  • Fresh parsley, to taste
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • Reserved pasta water, as needed
  • A small squeeze of lemon

Instructions

  1. Peel the garlic cloves and place them in a small baking dish. Fully cover with olive oil. Bake at 350°F (180°C) for 45–50 minutes, until soft, golden, and spreadable.
  2. Thinly slice the onion. Cook it in the butter with a pinch of salt over medium-low heat for 15–20 minutes, until soft, golden, and caramelized.
  3. Meanwhile, cook the pasta until al dente. Reserve plenty of pasta water before draining.
  4. Add the garlic confit, a little garlic oil, chili flakes or chili paste, sun-dried tomatoes, Parmesan, fresh parsley, a splash of pasta water, black pepper, and a small squeeze of lemon to a blender.
  5. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  6. Pour the sauce into the pan with the caramelized onions and stir together.
  7. Add the cooked pasta and toss until completely coated. Add more pasta water as needed until the sauce is glossy, silky, and creamy.
  8. Finish with extra Parmesan, fresh parsley, and black pepper.
